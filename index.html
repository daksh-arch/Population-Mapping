Density extremes

The Ganges Plain

Stretching from Pakistan to Bangladesh, the Indo-Gangetic Plain is home to over 1 billion people — packed into a strip of land smaller than Argentina. The densest sustained region on Earth.

Density extremes

The empty half

The Sahara, Siberia, the Amazon, and the Australian outback together cover 30% of Earth's land area but hold fewer than 1% of its people. Population is not distributed — it's clustered.

Regional spotlight

The Pearl River Delta

Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Hong Kong, Macau — the Pearl River Delta megalopolis houses 86 million people in a continuous urban mass. In 1980, most of it was farmland.

Regional spotlight

Java

The island of Java — smaller than England — is home to 157 million people, making it the most populous island on Earth. Jakarta alone holds 11 million in its city limits.

Regional spotlight

The Nile Delta

Egypt is 96% desert. Nearly all of its 105 million people live in a thin ribbon along the Nile and its delta — one of the sharpest population edges visible from space.

Historical context

A century of growth

In 1927, Earth held 2 billion people. By 2011 it was 7 billion. By 2024, 8 billion. The spikes you see are not just geography — they're the result of a century of explosive urbanisation.

Nearly 60% of people now live in cities. In 1950, that number was 30%.
